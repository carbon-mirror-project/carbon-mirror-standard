# Code of Conduct  
Carbon Mirror Project

The Carbon Mirror Project is an open standard dedicated to improving the clarity, efficiency, and energy literacy of digital systems.  
To maintain a healthy, respectful, and productive environment for all contributors, the following Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub discussions, issues, pull requests, RFCs, and any related forums.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant (v2.1) and tailored for an open standards community.

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and maintainers of the Carbon Mirror Project, pledge to:

- Foster an inclusive, respectful, and harassment-free community  
- Encourage thoughtful, evidence-based discussion  
- Support collaboration in an environment grounded in curiosity and mutual respect  
- Uphold the integrity and neutrality of the Standard  

We commit to making participation a positive experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age  
- Body size  
- Visible or invisible disability  
- Ethnicity or national origin  
- Level of experience  
- Gender identity or expression  
- Sexual orientation  
- Socioeconomic background  
- Religion or affiliation  

---

## 2. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Engage respectfully and constructively  
- Offer and receive feedback graciously  
- Base arguments on evidence, clarity, and reasoning  
- Keep discussions focused on improving the Standard  
- Assume good intent while allowing space for correction  
- Welcome new contributors and questions  

Participants should strive to:

- Use clear, accessible language  
- Ask questions before assuming context  
- Acknowledge differing viewpoints  
- Help uphold project quality without hostility  

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

### Harassment and Abuse
- Insults, demeaning comments, or personal attacks  
- Threats of violence or intimidation  
- Sexualized language or imagery  
- Unwanted advances or inappropriate contact  

### Discrimination  
- Derogatory comments based on personal characteristics  
- Exclusionary language or behavior  

### Disruptive Conduct
- Trolling, inflammatory comments, or deliberate provocation  
- Persistent repetition of rejected ideas  
- Derailing discussions or PR reviews  
- Introducing vendor marketing or promotional content into the Standard  
- Attempts to dominate or intimidate other contributors  

### Misrepresentation
- Presenting personal opinions as official Standard positions  
- Misstating the project's intentions or authority  

---

## 4. Responsibilities of Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing contributions civily and impartially  
- Guiding discussions toward clarity and resolution  
- Enforcing the Code of Conduct when needed  
- Taking corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior  

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject contributions (issues, comments, PRs) that violate this Code.

---

## 5. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub repositories under the Carbon Mirror Project  
- Public discussions  
- Contribution review workflows  
- RFC discussions  
- Any official or semi-official communication channels used by the project

## 7. Enforcement Actions

Depending on the severity, maintainers may take any of the following actions:

### Mild
- Formal warning  
- Request to modify or retract a comment  

### Moderate
- Temporary participation limits (e.g., restricted commenting)  
- PR review restrictions  

### Severe
- Temporary or permanent ban from project spaces  
- Rejection of current and future contributions  

Severe enforcement actions require consensus among maintainers.

---

## 8. Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was unjustified:

- You may request an appeal by emailing the maintainers  
- Appeals must include clear reasoning and relevant context  
- Maintainers will review the appeal and respond with a final decision  

Appeals are decided by maintainer consensus.

## The Point

The purpose of this Code is simple:

To maintain a community where ideas are evaluated on their merit,  
where contributors treat one another with respect,  
and where the Standard can grow with clarity, discipline, and integrity.
