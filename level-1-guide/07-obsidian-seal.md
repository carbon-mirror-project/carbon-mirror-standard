# The Obsidian Seal  
### (The Mark of Verified Operational Discipline)

## Why the Seal Exists

Every serious standard needs a visible marker.  
Not as decoration.  
As **signal**.

The Obsidian Seal is the public mark of full compliance with the Carbon Mirror Standard.  
It is awarded only when an organization demonstrates:

- Measurable adherence to the four Mirrors  
- Sustained, verifiable operational behavior  
- Transparent accounting of invisible system costs  
- A mature, energy-literate digital architecture  

Claims are cheap.  
Proof is rare.  
The Seal exists to distinguish one from the other.

---

## What the Seal Means

The Obsidian Seal communicates four things simultaneously:

### **1. Measured Reality**
The organization has quantified the real operational shape of its digital systems, including:

- Model routing distributions  
- Time-aligned workload behavior  
- Data weight dynamics  
- Infrastructure byproduct patterns  

### **2. Intentional Alignment**
The organization has tuned its systems to behave with:

- Efficiency  
- Timing literacy  
- Payload discipline  
- Circular design awareness  

### **3. Sustained Discipline**
Improvements are not one-time optimizations.  
They are embedded in architecture and culture.

### **4. External Verification**
An independent reviewer has confirmed that claims match reality.

The Seal does **not** mean perfection.  
It means **accountable, durable alignment**.

## How the Seal Is Earned

Organizations achieve the Obsidian Seal by demonstrating:

### **1. Verified Mirror Alignment**
Across all four Mirrors:

- Efficiency  
- Timing  
- Data Diet  
- Circularity  

Each must show measurable, sustained improvement.

### **2. 12 Months of Sustained Indicators**
Organizations must show at least one year of data reflecting:

- Mirror-aligned model routing behavior  
- Time-flexible workloads scheduled in clean windows  
- Reduced payload mass and duplication  
- Improved waste-to-yield infrastructure ratios  

### **3. Automated Measurement**
Manual sampling is not enough.  
Level 3 requires:

- Automated reporting  
- Instrumented pipelines  
- Transparent metrics  

### **4. Third-Party Review**
Independent auditors review:

- Metrics  
- Workload samples  
- Architectural patterns  
- Operational procedures  
- Documentation  

Only when mirrors, metrics, and audits align is the Seal awarded.
